Assets Overview

Ice Europetrol has been producing oil in Albania since 2012.

Ice Europetrol uses environmentally friendly primary high standard production methods and applies leading western technology to leveraging productivity in oilfields which have historically underperformed due to the use of outdated equipment and operating methodologies. The Visoka Field has been operated by a subsidiary of Ice Europetrol since February 2012 under a production sharing agreement with the Albanian government. The Company has invested over USD 80 million in capital expenditures and operations since 2012. Upon finalization and signing of the production sharing agreements for additional fields with existing production located in Cakran-Mollaj, Gorisht and Amonicë, will hold 100% interest in four onshore oil fields in Albania. The producing assets, located in the south of the country, deliver medium to heavy oil from fractured carbonate reservoirs.

Ice Europetrol executed the heads of terms with the Albanian government for the Cakran-Mollaj, Gorisht and Amonicë fields in 2018, under comparable terms to Visoka. Current production totals approximately 1,100 bopd across all four oil fields.

Once the production sharing agreements for the new fields have been finalized and signed, Ice Europetrol’s combined 2P reserves for the four fields is estimated (by management) at 54 mmbbl. This could be massively augmented by material development upsides and acquisition opportunities. The Company aims to raise total production from 1,100 bopd to over 20,000 bopd. This will be implemented by investing in new technology and state-of-the-art processes which, given the historical underdevelopment of these assets, is expected to provide significant returns.

The wide-ranging and comprehensive experience of Ice Europetrol’s management team will be utilised to ensure optimal production, operational and financial excellence. The team’s experience includes heavy oil development through to production, success in managing operations with legacy Soviet technology and upgrading former Communist era management processes.

Ice Europetrol’s assets benefit from proximity to developed infrastructure. Being close to Fier, Albania’s sixth most populous city, road infrastructure and neighbouring towns provide support for the Company’s oilfields and employees. Ice Europetrol currently transports all of its crude oil production to local refineries via truck. Albania has two refineries, Ballsh and Fier, with refining capacities of 6 million barrels and 3 million barrels, respectively. Crude oil can also be transported via truck to Albania’s two oil export seaports, Porto Romano and Petrolifera Italo Albanese, where the oil can be exported to Italy or other refineries globally. The ports are suitable for international cargo sizes of 120,000 – 180,000 bbls of oil.

Albania

Geology

Albania is located in the south-eastern part of the Adriatic basin. Present day topography is influenced by its location along the Dinaric-Albanic-Hellenic arc of the Alpine orogeny, which resulted from the tertiary collision of the African, Adriatic (Apulian) and Eurasian Plates. Albania occupies the deformed eastern Apulian (Tethyan) margin and contains Triassic–Eocene carbonate successions. The architecture of Albania shows a conventional fold and thrust belt divided into distinct geological provinces. Ice Europetrol’s focus is on the outer Albanides in southern Albania (Ionian Zone), which are comprised of frontal thrust and foothill structures involving Mesozoic rocks formed as part of the Tethyan passive margin.

Operating Environment

Albania provides a strong stable operating environment for oil companies. It was accepted as an EU candidate in 2014. Its EU application has made substantial progress and, in the meantime, the country already receives EU development subsidies. It has been a NATO member since 2009 and an ICC member since 2003. With its first multiparty election in 1991, the country became a democracy. Since then, the government is robust. Peaceful elections are run regularly. The country is known for its stable legal framework and employs international arbitration procedures under UNCITRAL rules.

Albania has developed a healthy economy, which is benefitting from far-reaching structural changes being implemented by the government. The GDP growth rate has exceeded 3% since 2016 and is expected to remain strong, according to a World Bank analysis. In the 2017 Index of Economic Freedom, Albania ranked 13th in the world in terms of trade freedom. It has been a free market economy since communist domination ended in 1991.

Visoka

The Visoka oil field began production in 1963. Cumulative production amounts to over 35 mmbbl (4.5% of mid-case original oil in place estimate of 769 mmbbl) of 10 to 12 API gravity oil. Reservoir pressure is supported by an underlying aquifer. The field was developed historically with over 240 vertical wells. Ice Europetrol has drilled 12 additional horizontal wells, which demonstrate substantial improvement over vertical wells, and provide the data on which to form the basis for an upgraded development plan to drill up to 170 additional oil wells.

There are approximately 98 active wells and water injection has been shown to be beneficial in the Visoka reservoir. An advanced and well planned water injection program is expected to be rolled out during further development over the next two to three years. In the meantime, existing production is being augmented through well intervention work including pump replacements and a nitrogen stimulation program which is showing early but strong signs of success.

Cakran-Mollaj

The Cakran-Mollaj Field will be the deepest reservoir of the four fields and started production in 1978. The field has produced over 29 mmbbl of oil with an average 27 API gravity, achieving a maximum oil production rate of over 14,000 bopd. Ice Europetrol estimates the original oil in-place volume to be approximately 568 mmbbl. Cakran-Mollaj also has a primary gas cap providing additional drive energy and potential for a gas development project.

Gorisht

The Gorisht Field started oil production in 1966 and has produced over 70 mmbbl of oil with an average 16 API gravity, achieving a maximum oil production rate of nearly 10,000 bopd from 295 wells. Ice Europetrol estimates the oil in-place volume to be approximately 720 mmbbl. Water injection has also been shown to be beneficial in this reservoir, so further development plans include drilling horizontal wells and implementing a water injection program.

Amonicë

Since commencing oil production in 1981, the Amonicë Field achieved a maximum oil production rate of over 3,000 bopd from 67 wells. It has produced over 5 mmbbl of oil, with an average 25 API gravity. The estimated original oil in-place volume is approximately 104 mmbbl.
